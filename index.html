The Writer
I've spent the last six years learning that the browser is a stage for craft — and that the most interesting frontend problems rarely come from the framework layer. They come from the pixels, the bytes, and the seconds the user has to wait.

Today I'm an SSE-1 at Aftershoot, leading frontend on a gallery product where a single wedding photographer might upload 2,000 images and expect their client to browse them on a phone at the reception. The constraints are delicious: upload pipelines, retry semantics, virtualization, progressive decoding, pixel-perfect layouts that don't shift.

Before that I spent three and a half years at Delhivery building DispatchOne — their productized dispatch-management SaaS — including the Orca-UI design system, the web dashboard's Routing and Workflow surfaces, and the Driver mobile app. Then a year at Zeta architecting a Shadow-DOM component platform with runtime federation and SSR host hooks.

I'm happiest when I'm turning vague constraints into discoverable systems — and when the thing I ship feels like it was made, not assembled.
